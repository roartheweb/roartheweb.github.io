What are Wordpress and Static file websites?

Wordpress
Wordpress (WP) is an extremely popular way to run a website. Almost 25% of all websites are WP-based. Here are the PRO's and CON's for going with WP:
  • Pro: It is dynamic. That means it deals with user databases. This lets you create user profiles, write blogs and even build your own social network!
    • Con: The database-based frameworks are generally "heavier"- meaning they load slower then static file sites. There are ways to improve on this, but still they won't be as speedy as static file.
  • Pro: Has a very user-friendly interface, so you'll never have to see the code behind it.          
  • Pro: Many plugins (free and $) are available- ranging from forms and newsletter extensions to e-commerce sites. They are easy to install.           
  • Pro: Free and paid themes pre-written for it and actively updated.          
  • Pro: Free and paid plugins are available to customize WP on top of the themes.                
    • Con: The built-in and plugin-based customization options only go as far as the theme developer and WP framework allow.
  • Pro: Many other plugins (free and $) are available- ranging from forms and newsletter extensions to e-commerce sites. They are easy to install.           

Static file websites
Static files literally means the page is composed of HTML, CSS and Javascript. As the site does not need to call the database every so often, pages load very fast. 
  • Pro: We leave in an era of mobile traffic. Literally, 70% of the internet is now mobile, and the number of new mobile users is growing at 25% per year. In terms of speed (and more importantly- pricing), mobile networks still fall far behind desktop. Having a very speedy (static) site means three benefits:
    • Firstly, the users are more likely to stay if the site loads quicker (a great amount of research is devoted to this). If the connection isn't that great (Mountains/ Rural areas/ Man-made interference), slow sites can become a pain as well.
    • Secondly, a lot of people have a fixed monthly download limit (paying a lot outside of it). May not be so noticeable for those with unlimited data, but heavy sites will bite the pocket of most people.
    • Thirdly, search engines (Google, Bing) do promote websites based on load times. This means that a snappier site will display a page or two earlier compared to a bulkier one.
  • Pro: Ultimate customization options. I mean indisputably ultimate- ranging from design to the type of navigation to special animation/ parallax effects to how the page will look printed.                        
  • Pro: Ultimate customization options. I mean indisputably ultimate- ranging from design to the type of navigation to special animation/ parallax effects to how the page will look printed.                  
    • Con: If you would like to edit your website after completion and you are not familiar with html/ css/ js- it can get tricky.
      • Pro: Fortunately, there are a variety of front-end editors that I can include with different amounts of complexity to them. Front-end editing means you'd be able to open your site as you normally would, then click on any element and change it's contents. Big-scale design changes would reqiure a web developer- but that goes for any other database system as well.
  • Pro: Greater security. Compared to database-driven systems, there are not as many "weak spots" that a hacker/ virus can exploit.                  

Why do I even need a website?

Stand out
We live in 21st century, when all the phonebooks live in the cloud. Having your Brand VISIBLE and ACESSIBLE means having a significant online footprint.
  • Example: Imagine you want to take your friends out for a dinner.
    • Background: You've got some nice places you like, but you're into trying out a new place.
    • Action: What are you reaching for? Your cell phone/ tablet/ laptop !
    • A bit more Action: You search the restaurants in proximity, read the reviews, look at the pictures and the menu. Then you send a link to your friends. Perhaps even book it online.
    • Result: And the restaurant next block just lost a bunch of customers
  • Conclusion: An old-school word of mouth is a great promotion tool, but being online gives a more robust way of connecting to customers and generating revenue.
  • Facts: A survey by Yelp concluded that nearly 85% of consumers use internet as a tool for finding businesses.    

Trust thy Brand
The world is getting more inter-connected. Consumers rely on other consumers' opinions when evaluating the Pro's and Con's of your product.
  • Brand Perception: Having a modern, responsive website is like wearing a well-tailored business suit. Like having a professionally designed logo and a business card. Both send out a clear message to a customer- "Our brand is committed to providing quality products and servies".
  • Perception Exposure: 88% of customers rely on the online reviews to determine Brand Quality (based on a study by BrightLogical). That includes reviews on Yelp, Google Reviews, TripAdvisor, Zomato as well as to peer-to-peer social networks (Facebook, Instagram, Twitter). People usually link activelly and follow links. Being actively online lets you tap into these perception-forming resourses. Having a well designed site with a cogent domain name lets you follow up nicely on the trust that goes into clicking a link.
  • Conquering new markets (desktop): Having an eCommerce web-based store is a happy ending on the review-link-website chain we looked at above. It is the way of monetizing on your online presence.
  • Conquering new markets (mobile): eCommerce has a bright future on mobile too- with the advent of Google Wallet and ApplePay (and other big names following up). Currently, Mobile offers a more unique opportunity- call/ email/ text shortcut buttons. They take users directly to their native OS call/text apps or an email app with a tailored email-friendly keyboard inside. This easiness makes users more likely to call you (some companies quote of up to 200% lead increase over the phone). Having a well-designed site would mean a client who's more informed, saving your time. Plus, he/she just gave you their mobile number!
  • Brand involvment: Keep your customers up-to date with newsletters, rss feeds, social network promotions and surveys. These are not only usefull sales and marketing tools, these are valuable statistical analysis instruments. They will both capture existing customers into prolongued relationship with your brand and will let you adjust your sales startegies by giving you meaningfull customer feedback.
